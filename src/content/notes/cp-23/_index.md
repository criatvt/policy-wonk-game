# Demand & Supply

This module is the standard apparatus of microeconomic thinking: how demand curves and supply curves describe what consumers and producers want at different prices, how markets find equilibrium, and how the equilibrium shifts when one or both curves move. The framework is old, well-tested, and useful enough that policy practitioners across the political spectrum still reach for it.

The case material this module draws on is heavily Indian and recent: hand sanitiser shortages during COVID, the egg-price spikes of 2020, the rescue-puppy market boom, and the recurring debates over price controls. Each case illustrates how the textbook apparatus actually works (and where it gets complicated) in real markets where elasticities are messy, expectations matter, and policy interventions interact with underlying supply and demand in ways the curves don't immediately capture.

Topics in this module:

- **Demand fundamentals** — the law of demand, what shifts the demand curve, normal goods and inferior goods, substitutes and complements.
- **Supply fundamentals** — the law of supply, what shifts the supply curve, the role of expectations.
- **Equilibrium** — how prices and quantities settle, what happens during shortages and surpluses, why market clearing matters.
- **Elasticity** — measuring responsiveness, tax incidence, cross-price elasticity, income elasticity, and the short-run vs long-run distinction.
- **Price controls** — ceilings, floors, and the non-price rationing mechanisms that show up when prices are not allowed to clear.
- **Market dynamics and shocks** — supply shocks, demand surges, and how elasticity determines whether shocks show up as price changes or quantity changes.
- **Special cases** — Giffen goods, Veblen goods, income and substitution effects, and the limits of the textbook model.
